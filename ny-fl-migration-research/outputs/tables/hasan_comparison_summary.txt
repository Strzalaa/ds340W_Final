GLM Poisson comparison (389 NY→FL dyads; offset = log(origin population); continuous predictors z-scored like the main Layer-2 spec). The full model with LightGBM-derived dyadic_housing_gap and dyadic_affordability_gap (plus rent dummies and abs_diff_affordability_index) achieves deviance 10820.73 and AIC 13173.93. The same gravity and politics core with only raw ACS dyadic differences—absolute gaps in median home value, median gross rent, and percent owner-occupied—yields deviance 11818.03 and AIC 14167.23, about 997 points worse than the ML specification, so the ML layer adds clear explanatory power beyond tabular Census housing differences alone. A politics-and-gravity-only model with no housing variables (matching the no_housing knockout) has deviance 12248.03; relative to that baseline, raw ACS housing recovers roughly 430 points of deviance while the ML full model recovers about 1427 points—i.e., raw ACS captures a substantial share of the housing signal but not all of what the boosted predictions contribute in this corridor.
